# Brand Guidelines
**FitByte — Brand Voice & Content Standards**

---

## Who We Are

FitByte is a fitness watch built for people who are serious about their health — but don't want a device that makes them feel like they're in a lab.

We sit at the intersection of performance technology and everyday life. Our wearers range from competitive athletes tracking race-day splits to busy professionals who want to close their rings and get on with their day. What they share is a belief that data — the right data, presented simply — can help them live and move better.

Our voice reflects that. Smart. Grounded. A little bit gritty.

---

## Our Voice

### Clear, not clinical
We deal in data — heart rate zones, sleep stages, recovery scores — but we never talk like a research paper. If a stat needs a sentence of context to be useful, we write that sentence. Plain language always wins.

### Motivating, not preachy
We believe in our users. We celebrate progress, not perfection. We don't lecture people about their health choices. We give them the information to make their own.

### Precise, not verbose
Every word earns its place. Short sentences. Strong verbs. No filler. No fluff. If you can cut it without losing meaning, cut it.

### Confident, not arrogant
FitByte has the data to back up what we say. We don't need to shout. We state our product's capabilities clearly, let the specs speak for themselves, and trust our users to see the difference.

### Human, not robotic
We're a tech company that makes something people wear on their bodies. That's intimate. Our voice should feel like it comes from a person — warm, direct and occasionally funny — not a firmware update.

## Our Audience

### The Performance Athlete
**Profile:** Runners, cyclists, triathletes, swimmers — training with purpose and tracking every metric
**What they want:** Accurate split times, VO2 max, training load, race predictions, recovery guidance
**What they don't want:** Battery life anxiety, a cluttered interface, vague data

### The Fitness Enthusiast
**Profile:** Gym regulars, hikers, weekend warriors — active but not competing
**What they want:** Workout tracking, heart rate zones, streak motivation, sleep quality
**What they don't want:** Overwhelming complexity, jargon they didn't ask for

### The Health-Conscious Professional
**Profile:** 30–50, desk job, deliberately active — uses fitness to counterbalance a sedentary workday
**What they want:** Stress tracking, move reminders, sleep insights, clean design that works in meetings
**What they don't want:** Sporty aesthetics that look out of place at the office

### The Upgrader
**Profile:** Coming off a basic fitness tracker or a competitor watch
**What they want:** Proof that FitByte is worth the switch — better accuracy, longer battery, smarter insights
**What they don't want:** A learning curve, a cluttered migration process, features they'll never use

## Writing Rules

### Active voice — always
| ✅ Use | ❌ Avoid |
|---|---|
| FitByte tracks your heart rate continuously. | Your heart rate is tracked continuously by FitByte. |
| The sensor detects sleep stages automatically. | Sleep stages are automatically detected. |

### Lead with the benefit
Features are only interesting because of what they do for the user. Always connect a capability to an outcome.

| ❌ Feature-first | ✅ Benefit-first |
|---|---|
| "Dual-frequency GPS." | "Dual-frequency GPS locks on in seconds — even in dense cities." |
| "Continuous SpO2 monitoring." | "SpO2 monitoring runs all night, so you wake up knowing how well you actually recovered." |

### Precision over hype
Data is our product. Don't inflate claims.

| ❌ Avoid | ✅ Use instead |
|---|---|
| "The most accurate fitness watch on the market." | "Accurate to within 1–2 BPM during high-intensity exercise." |
| "Incredible battery life." | "Up to 14 days on a single charge." |
| "Revolutionary sleep tracking." | "Six-stage sleep tracking that goes beyond light and deep." |

### No serial comma
- ❌ "track your heart rate, sleep, and stress"
- ✅ "track your heart rate, sleep and stress"

### Em dash
Use em dashes to break up sentences and add punch — like this. (Mac shortcut: `Option + Shift + -`)

### Numbers
- Always use numerals: "7 days," "14 hours," "3 BPM" — not "seven days"
- Include units: "14-day battery," "50m water resistance," "1–2 BPM accuracy"
- Round generously for marketing copy: "up to 14 days" not "336 hours"

## Words We Use — and Words We Don't

| ✅ Use | ❌ Don't Use |
|---|---|
| fitness watch | smartwatch (we're sport-first) |
| activity | exercise session |
| insights | analytics (too corporate) |
| recovery score | recuperation index |
| strain | exertion level |
| sleep stages | sleep cycles (imprecise) |
| workout | training session (unless athlete audience) |
| FitByte app | the application |
| zone (heart rate zone) | HR band |
| all-day tracking | 24/7 monitoring (clinical) |
| water-resistant | waterproof (we don't claim this) |
| up to X days | X-day battery (avoid hard promises) |
| athletes | sports people |
| move reminder | sedentary alert (negative framing) |

## Tone by Audience

| Audience | Tone | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Performance athlete | Technical, precise, peer-to-peer | "VO2 max is recalibrated after every outdoor run above 20 minutes." |
| Fitness enthusiast | Encouraging, clear, energetic | "Every rep counts. FitByte makes sure none of them go untracked." |
| Health-conscious professional | Calm, smart, design-aware | "Looks like a watch. Works like a health coach." |
| Retail / shelf copy | Direct, punchy, benefit-led | "Track more. Worry less." |
| Support / help docs | Neutral, step-by-step, jargon-free | "To reset your device, press and hold the side button for 8 seconds." |

---

## Tone by Channel

| Channel | Tone | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Product page | Confident, benefit-led | Specs paired with context; avoid raw feature dumps |
| App UI | Brief, encouraging, clear | Max 10 words per prompt; celebrate milestones |
| Social (Instagram) | Visual-first, punchy captions | 1–2 lines max; lead with the hook |
| Social (LinkedIn) | Credible, data-led | Health tech angle; workplace wellness crossover |
| Email campaigns | Direct, personalized | Subject lines under 45 characters; one CTA per email |
| Press releases | Neutral, factual | Superlatives in quotes only |
| Support docs | Neutral, step-by-step | Numbered instructions; no jargon without definition |
| Packaging | Minimal, bold | 5 words or fewer per claim |

## What We Never Do

- Shame users for inactivity, poor sleep or high stress scores
- Make medical claims or position FitByte as a diagnostic device
- Use fear-based messaging ("your heart might be at risk")
- Overpromise accuracy in ways we can't back up
- Use competitor names in marketing copy
- Write in passive voice
- Use the serial comma

---

## Brand Voice Cheat Sheet

### Do
- Lead with benefits, support with specs
- Use precise numbers over vague superlatives
- Write short sentences with strong verbs
- Match tone to channel and audience
- Celebrate progress, not perfection
- Sound like a person, not a product manual

### Don't
- Use passive voice
- Use the serial comma
- Make unqualified medical claims
- Use hype words without data to back them up
- Shame or pressure users
- Bury the lead
